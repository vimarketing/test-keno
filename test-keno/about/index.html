What we do
The brief, in five sentences.

We build lottery titles — Keno draws, jackpot wheels, bingo verticals — that ship into operator lobbies in four weeks, not twelve.

We do this from native teams in Belgrade, Zagreb, and Bucharest, because Balkan regulator stacks, locale conventions, and player culture do not transcreate from a London or Stockholm desk.

We sell on revenue-share, not minimum guarantees, because mid-market operator unit economics break under €100K MGs that legacy vendors quote as standard.

We treat compliance — WLA RG L1, GLI-19, MGA B2B Critical Supply — as a Day-3 deliverable, not a Week-4 add-on. The pack is in your compliance officer's inbox before sandbox access.

And we name the integration engineer on the kickoff document, because "we'll assign someone" is the seven-word spell that turns four weeks into twelve.

How we work

Four operating principles. Visible from the kickoff doc.

Marketing language gets confused with operating reality often in this category. These four are observable on Day 1 — not promises that have to be earned later.

Principle 01

Named integration engineer per account

One named engineer owns each integration from Week 0 through Day-30 post-go-live. Not a pool, not a rotation. The name is on the kickoff document and the operator's Slack from Day 1.

Principle 02

Compliance pack as Day-3 deliverable

WLA RG L1, GLI-19, MGA B2B, and locale-mapping documentation arrive as a single PDF in the operator's compliance officer's inbox within three business days of LOI. Not Week 4.

Principle 03

Revenue-share, no MG, 12-month renewable

Commercial template fits mid-market unit economics by design — not adapted from a tier-1 enterprise lottery contract. Legal redline runs in 3-5 business days, not 3-5 weeks.

Principle 04

Mobile-first against the bottom-quartile device

Engineering target is sustained 60fps on a Xiaomi Redmi 10 and Samsung Galaxy A12 — not on the studio's flagship Pixel. Asset payload <4MB initial / <12MB total. Tested on the device the player actually owns.
