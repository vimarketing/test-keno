What we mean

What aggregator-channel-ready actually means.

Three things, simultaneously. Get one wrong and the deal stalls. Get all three right and the catalog math works.

i.

RGS handshake — without a custom layer

Bingo's RGS architecture is engineered against the same platform-handshake patterns EveryMatrix, SoftSwiss, Pariplay, and Relax already run. Platform integration completes in 4-6 weeks at the platform level — not 12, not 16. The aggregator's platform engineering team does not need to build a Bingo-specific integration layer.

ii.

Commercial model — fits aggregator unit economics

Revenue-share at 12-18% of net revenue, no minimum guarantee, no upfront integration fee, MDF-compatible, exclusivity windows negotiable. Commercial close in 4-6 weeks. Legal redline on standard templates in 3-5 business days. The studio's commercial templates are pre-built for aggregator stacks.

iii.

Per-title cert stack — supports platform-level cascade

GLI-19 per-title artifact, MGA B2B Critical Supply at studio level, WLA RG L1 in the title UI. The aggregator's platform RGS handles the certification pass-through once. The downstream operator does not separately certify each new Bingo title against their own platform's RGS layer.

iv.

Catalog scale — 90-ball, 75-ball, jackpot variant

Three Bingo SKUs share a single engine and a single RGS handshake. The aggregator gets three catalog tiles for one platform integration. Operators downstream pick variants per market — 90-ball UK-style, 75-ball US-style, jackpot tier where regulator allows.

For catalog acquisition teams

FAQ — Bingo for catalog teams.

What is the typical revenue-share for aggregator-channel bingo content?
12-18% of net revenue to the studio, after the operator's cut and before the aggregator's. The exact rate depends on co-branded versus white-label deployment, MDF commitment, and exclusivity windows. White-label commercial terms typically run 30-50% below co-branded because the studio loses brand-equity-build.
How does GLI-19 certification cascade work in aggregator-channel distribution?
The studio holds the per-title GLI-19 certification artifact. The aggregator's platform handles the RGS certification once at the platform level, so the operator does not separately certify each new bingo title against their own platform's RGS layer. The operator still files the regulator-facing artifact for each title, but the technical certification is handled at the studio and platform layers.
What is the realistic timeline from aggregator LOI to first operator-side go-live?
8-12 weeks from LOI to first operator-side go-live for a studio built for aggregator-channel distribution. That is 4-6 weeks for platform-level integration, 2-4 weeks for commercial finalisation in parallel, and 2-4 weeks for the first operator activation in their lobby. Studios not built for aggregator-channel typically double this timeline.
Why should aggregators close the lottery-vertical gap in 2026-2027 specifically?
The 12-18 month window exists because mid-market lottery studios built for aggregator distribution are now available — supply has changed — and operator-side demand for lottery content is rising in the Balkans, CEE, and EU big-5. By 2028 the aggregator catalogs that are still 5%-or-less lottery will be losing operator-side RFPs to aggregators that moved earlier.
Can Bingo deploy co-branded with the studio attribution visible to end-players?
Yes — co-branded is the default. Co-branded deployments in regulated Balkan and CEE markets earn higher trust signals on the player-facing UI because end-players actively look at studio attribution as a trust marker. White-label is available on request, with commercial terms 30-50% below co-branded.
