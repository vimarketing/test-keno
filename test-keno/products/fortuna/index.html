Why now

2026 regulator audits don't accept "we're working on it."

The audit posture changed. The vendor lists did not.

Three regulators — MUP RS in Serbia, MinFin HR in Croatia, ONJN in Romania — moved within fourteen months on the same pattern: per-title certification artifact custody, RG features inside the title UI, and locale-correct compliance documentation.

Most lottery titles in market today were built against a previous era's spec, where compliance lived at the platform layer and "we are GLI-19 certified" was a suite-level statement.

In 2026, a CPO who answers a regulator letter that way is a CPO with a problem the next morning.

The mid-market operator running a Q3 lobby refresh has six weeks to file an audit-clean compliance pack with three regulators. The vendor whose certification artifact list is "in recertification" is not the vendor that gets onto the lobby this cycle.

What we mean

What "compliance-ready" actually means in Fortuna.

Four things. None of them are a roadmap promise.

i.

Per-title GLI-19 — on file before sandbox

Fortuna's GLI-19 ID, issue date, expiry date, and lab attribution are listed on the studio's compliance dashboard from the day a sandbox is granted. There is no "we'll have it by Week 3." Either the artifact is on file before Day 1, or the integration does not start.

ii.

WLA RG L1 — in the title UI, not the wallet

Reality-check prompts, deposit-limit hooks, self-exclusion handoffs, spend-tracking — they live in Fortuna's gameplay UI, in the same frame as the jackpot wheel. The audit checks the title layer. Fortuna passes the title layer.

iii.

MGA B2B Critical Supply — at the studio level

Studio-level licensing arrives in the operator's compliance officer's inbox within three business days of contract signature. Letterhead, expiry, scope. No follow-up emails required to confirm what's covered.

iv.

SR / HR / RO mapping — side-by-side, not separate

One regulator-mapping document covers MUP RS reporting fields, MinFin HR audit categories, and ONJN player-protection clauses individually. No three separate vendor briefs to reconcile. No locale revisions in Week 5.

For compliance teams

FAQ — Fortuna for compliance teams.

Is Fortuna certified for Serbia, Croatia, and Romania regulator filings?
Yes. Fortuna ships with GLI-19 per-title certification artifacts and meets MGA B2B Critical Supply requirements at the studio level. The studio provides MUP RS, MinFin HR, and ONJN regulator-mapping documentation as part of the compliance pack delivered before sandbox access.
What does WLA RG Level 1 mean in the Fortuna title specifically?
Fortuna includes reality-check prompts, deposit-limit hooks, self-exclusion handoffs, and spend-tracking inside the same UI as the jackpot reveal — not redirected to a separate wallet portal. This is the WLA Responsible Gaming Framework Level 1 spec applied at the title layer rather than the platform layer.
How long does Fortuna integration take from sandbox to live?
Four weeks from sandbox access to operator-side go-live, when the operator has DPA, SLA, and revenue-share template ready for redline within the first 72 hours. Six weeks is the buffer-included version that absorbs one compliance surprise without slipping the go-live.
What is the revenue-share for Fortuna?
Revenue-share runs 12–18% of net revenue to the studio, after the operator's cut. The exact rate depends on co-branded versus white-label deployment, exclusivity windows, and MDF allocation. Co-branded deployments in Balkan markets typically settle at the upper end of the range.
Can Fortuna be deployed via aggregator or only studio-direct?
Both. Fortuna is available studio-direct to mid-market operators with full co-branding and 4-week integration, and via EU aggregator catalogs (EveryMatrix, SoftSwiss, Pariplay, Relax) where the platform-level RGS handshake shortens operator-side integration to 2-3 weeks.
