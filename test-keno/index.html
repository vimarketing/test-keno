Mobile performance is the lottery bottleneck in 2026 — and most operators do not know it.

A mid-market Balkan operator pulls D1 retention by device-tier. The product team's first instinct is "lower-LTV segment." That instinct is wrong. The retention gap is a performance signal.

A fast-cycle number-pick lottery with a 4-minute draw cadence, designed for high-frequency-of-play mobile sessions. Players pick 1–10 numbers from an 80-number pool. RNG is GLI-19 certified. Draw animation is engineered to hold 60fps on a Redmi 10 — the moment the title most often fails on mobile-second competitors.

FAQ — Keno for mobile product teams.

What frame rate does Keno sustain on a mid-tier Android device?

60 frames per second sustained on a Xiaomi Redmi 10 and Samsung Galaxy A12 — the bottom-quartile devices common in Balkan operator player bases. Frame rate does not drop below 50fps during draw animations or jackpot reveals, which is the threshold below which players read stutter as "the game is broken" and D1 retention drops 15-30%.

Is Keno PWA-installable, and does that matter?

Yes — Keno ships with a tested service worker, correct manifest, passing Chrome installability audit, and icon set scaled for Xiaomi MIUI, Samsung One UI, and stock Android launcher densities. PWA-installed players have a measurably different D30 retention curve from browser-only players, which makes installability one of the larger hidden levers in mobile lottery.

How does Keno compare to native Android apps in the Balkans?

PWA wins on speed of update, cross-OS coverage, lower install friction, and avoiding Play Store policy issues around real-money gaming. Native wins on push-notification reliability and deeper device APIs. The pragmatic answer for most mid-market Balkan operators is PWA-first with a future native-app option for high-LTV segments — and Keno's PWA architecture supports this without future re-engineering.

How can an operator verify the mobile performance claims before signing?

Three filters. First, the studio publishes a Mobile Performance Snapshot quarterly with frame-rate-on-Redmi-10 benchmarks per title. Second, a live demo is provided on a borrowed Redmi 10 — not a flagship Pixel. Third, a Chrome DevTools performance trace on a thirty-minute live session is delivered on request within five business days.

What is the single biggest mistake operators make evaluating mobile lottery performance?

Testing on the wrong device. The product team uses a flagship iPhone or Pixel, sees a smooth experience, and signs off — but the Balkan player base is not on those devices. The first hour of every mobile-lottery vendor evaluation should be on a borrowed mid-tier Android device, with the QA pass explicitly targeting the bottom-quartile of the operator's actual device-mix.
